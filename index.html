Campus Life

school library

built in 1992

Students are encouraged to borrow during Library lessons, class borrowing times, at lunchtime openings and before or after school. They can borrow up to 4 items at one time for a period of 2 weeks. Students with long-term overdue, damaged or lost books will be required to pay the cost of replacing the items. Please respect our resources so others can enjoy them after you. If there are books you are interested in reading or you think others will enjoy, please fill in the Library Request book near the front door.

Curriculum

Literacy

The study of English is central to the learning and development of all young Australians. It helps create confident communicators, imaginative thinkers and informed citizens. It is through the study of English that individuals learn to analyse, understand, communicate and build relationships with others and with the world around them. The study of English helps young people develop the knowledge and skills needed for education, training and the workplace.

Numeracy

The Numeracy Program follows the Victorian Curriculum, using the content descriptors, achievement standards and work samples to guide curriculum planning. The three strands taught include – Number and Algebra, Measurement and Geometry and Statistics and Probability.

STEM

During STEM lessons, students will be guided to make their own discoveries and solve problems through a range of challenges, experiments and hands on activities. Lessons will explore the main areas of Science, while applying the scientific, design and engineering processes.

Visual Arts

The Art program offers a variety of materials and techniques for students in order for them to explore their creativity. The program includes drawing, painting, clay, construction, collage, modelling and textiles. Each class attends a fortnightly 90 minute lesson with a specialist Art teacher. Work is stored at school in a folio and that is sent home at the end of the year. Every two years the school has an exhibition which showcases the students’ artworks. The Art department also makes props for the concert and festivals that the school takes part in.

Performing Arts

What do we do in class? Discover how music works and how it makes us feel. Listen to and think about different kinds of music. Learn about elements of music like beat, rhythm, tone and melody. Explore ways of experiencing music through different instruments, voice and drama. Experience the craft of performance and stage environments. Show our learning and performance skills to a range of audiences. Use our skills and learning in performance to enrich our other school work.

Physical Education

The Physical Education and Sport program offers a broad and challenging program from Prep to Grade 6. The students are encouraged to be involved and participate to the best of their abilities. At Viewbank we encourage lots of sport focused clinics from visiting groups in the community to enhance students knowledge and ability in a wide range of sports as well as involvement in events such as the Hoop Time Basketball Competition.

Japanese

Konnichiwa! All students at Viewbank Primary School will study Japanese for one session a week. In a typical Japanese class, we cover some basic oral language skills and practice using it through song, repetition and role plays. In addition to oral language, older children will also begin learning about the writing system in Japan.

Kitchen

The SAKGP aims to teach Australian children positive food habits through fun, hands-on learning as they grow, harvest, prepare and share delicious and healthy food. This program is an integral part of our school as it allows for parents, volunteers, students and teachers to work together in order to create a more sustainable future in the form of pleasurable, food education.

What's New

  • New Year 5 & 6 Building

    Our students have many opportunities to develop their leadership skills at Viewbank Primary School. We have a boy and a girl school captain and vice captain, selected from the Grade 6 classes and elected by their peers. These students play pivotal roles in school assemblies, school tours and in a range of other public speaking and mentoring situations. They even get to be 'Principal For A Day' in August each year.

  • New Garden

    Viewbank Primary School is lucky enough to be taking part in the Victorian Government ResourceSmart Schools initiative. ResourceSmart AuSSI (Australian Sustainable Schools Initiative) Victoria helps schools benefit from embedding sustainability in everything they do. The aim is to reduce costs while giving students the opportunity to learn about sustainability in a tangible and realistic environment. Viewbank Primary School is going to take action to minimise waste, save energy and water, promote biodiversity and reduce greenhouse gas emissions. As we achieve each module we will gain a star certification. Our aim is to become a 5 star school. This is a long term goal and one which we will be working towards for the next 5+ years.
